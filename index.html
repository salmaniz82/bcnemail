October's Breast Cancer Awareness Month is a key 'moment' to highlight the signs and symptoms of primary breast cancer, and incurable metastatic breast cancer which some people may present with from the start, while for some others it may develop many years after treatment for primary breast cancer.

In our 2019 UK survey of people living with incurable metastatic breast cancer, (also known as secondary breast cancer), nearly one of four (24%) respondents who had previously been treated for the breast cancer had to visit their GP three or more times with symptoms, before being diagnosed with the return and spread of the disease.

 
 
 
 
We know that GPs are extremely busy, and that the COVID-19 pandemic has put unprecedented pressures on their time and resources.
 
This is why at Breast Cancer Now, we have created tools and information that will help GPs to feel confident in recognising the signs and symptoms of metastatic breast cancer in women as quickly as possible. A prompt diagnosis and access to treatment and support is crucial to giving them the best quality of life they deserve.
